# Twitter Thread Template: Group Stock Comparison

**Use when**: Comparing 3-20 stocks with common theme
**Thread length**: 8-12 tweets
**Charts needed**: 3-4 visualizations
**Focus**: Pattern recognition, relative rankings, what separates winners

---

## THREAD STRUCTURE - GROUP COMPARISON

### Tweet 1/10 - HOOK (Theme-Focused)
Make it about the PATTERN or OPPORTUNITY, not individual stocks.

**Hook Formulas:**
- **Scarcity**: "Only [X] TSX stocks meet [rare criteria]. Here are the top [Y]: 🧵"
- **Comparison**: "[Metric A] vs [Metric B]: I found [X] stocks that win on both 🧵"
- **Hidden pattern**: "I screened [X] stocks. [Y%] share this hidden trait: 🧵"
- **Performance gap**: "[Group A] returned [X]%. [Group B] returned [Y]%. Why the gap: 🧵"

```
Example:

Only 47 TSX stocks pay 5%+ yields AND grew dividends 10+ consecutive years.

I screened all 2,676 stocks.

These 5 also have payout ratios under 70% (sustainability).

The rankings: 🧵
```

---

### Tweet 2/10 - CONTEXT & CRITERIA
Why this theme matters + your selection methodology.

**Structure:**
1. Why NOW? (market conditions, investor need)
2. Selection criteria (3-5 bullet points)
3. Results (how many qualified, how you narrowed)

```
With GICs at 4.5% and bonds volatile, income investors need alternatives.

My criteria (from 2,676 TSX stocks):
✓ Dividend yield ≥ 5%
✓ 10+ years consecutive payouts
✓ Payout ratio < 70%
✓ Market cap > $5B

Result: 47 stocks qualified → Selected top 5 by total return
```

---

### Tweet 3/10 - THE LIST
Reveal your selections. Build anticipation.

**Formats:**
- Ranked list (#1-5)
- Grouped by tier (Top/Mid/Bottom)
- Categorized by sector or characteristic

```
The 5 stocks that made the cut:

1. Royal Bank (RY) - Finance
2. TD Bank (TD) - Finance
3. Enbridge (ENB) - Energy
4. Bank of Montreal (BMO) - Finance
5. Bank of Nova Scotia (BNS) - Finance

Notice: 4 of 5 are banks. Not an accident.
```

### Tweet 5/10 - INSIGHT 1: The Pattern
What do the top performers have in common?

**Look for:**
- Common industry/sector
- Similar business models
- Shared financial characteristics
- Historical events they all survived

```
Pattern #1: Oligopoly power

All 5 operate in markets with high barriers to entry:
• Canadian banking (protected by regulation)
• Energy pipelines (infrastructure monopolies)

Pricing power = Dividend sustainability

This isn't luck. It's structural advantage.
```

- Scatter plot (yield vs growth, risk vs return)

### Tweet 7/10 - INSIGHT 2: The Outlier
Who breaks the pattern and why?

**Highlight:**
- Highest/lowest on key metric
- Different sector or business model
- Unique strength or weakness
- Why it's interesting

```
The outlier: Enbridge (ENB)

Only non-bank in the top 5. Why?

• Highest yield (5.8%) due to energy sector discount
• Utility-like cash flows (long-term contracts)
• Energy transition risk → valuation compression

Trade-off: Higher yield today, sector headwinds tomorrow

Risk-tolerant investors may prefer it. Conservative investors? Stick to banks.
```

**Chart types:**
- Grouped bar chart (3-4 metrics)

- Radar/spider chart (overall profile)
- Small multiples

### Tweet 9/10 - INSIGHT 3: Risk & Trade-offs
No free lunch. What are you giving up?

**Address:**
- Sector concentration risk
- Valuation concerns
- Growth limitations
- Macro headwinds

```
Reality check: These aren't growth stocks.

For retirees seeking income? That's acceptable.
For growth seekers? Look elsewhere.
```

## KEY DIFFERENCES FROM INDIVIDUAL ANALYSIS

| Element | Group Comparison | Individual Deep Dive |
|---------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Hook** | Pattern/theme | Company-specific surprise |
| **Focus** | Relative comparison | Complete business story |
| **Charts** | 3-4 (comparative) | 5-7 (detailed) |
| **Depth per stock** | 2-3 facts each | Full analysis |
| **Insights** | What separates winners | Why THIS company wins |
| **Length** | 8-12 tweets | 12-18 tweets |
| **Tone** | Educational, framework | Narrative, storytelling |
| **CTA** | "Which would you pick?" | "What's your take on [Co]?" |

## CHART SELECTION GUIDE BY STOCK COUNT

### 3-7 Stocks (Sweet Spot)
**Chart 1**: Horizontal bar - primary metric ranking
**Chart 2**: Scatter plot - two-dimensional comparison
**Chart 3**: Grouped bars - multi-metric scorecard
**Optional 4th**: Performance over time (line chart)

### 8-15 Stocks (Sector Overview)
**Chart 1**: Top 10 ranking (drop bottom 5 for clarity)
**Chart 2**: Tiered view (top/mid/bottom terciles)
**Chart 3**: Sector breakdown (pie + bar chart)
**Optional 4th**: Distribution histogram

### 16+ Stocks (Market Scan)
**Chart 1**: Top 15 only (focus on winners)
**Chart 2**: Scatter with top 10 labeled, rest as dots
**Chart 3**: Sector/industry summary (not individual stocks)
**Don't try to show all stocks** - it won't be readable on mobile

## INSIGHTS TO LOOK FOR IN GROUP ANALYSIS

**Pattern Recognition:**
- 4 of 5 are in same sector → sector-level advantage
- All survived [crisis] → defensive characteristics
- Inverse relationship between [X] and [Y] → trade-offs
- 80/20 rule: Top 2 have 80% of [metric]

**Outlier Analysis:**
- One stock much higher/lower on key metric
- Different business model but similar results
- Same sector, divergent performance → execution matters
- New entrant vs established players

**Segmentation:**
- High yield / low growth vs low yield / high growth
- Large cap stability vs mid cap opportunities
- Canadian pure-play vs US exposure
- Cyclical vs defensive

**Risk Clustering:**
- Sector concentration
- Geographic concentration
- Correlated moves (all rise/fall together)
- Macro sensitivities (rates, oil, FX)

## TONE & STYLE FOR GROUP THREADS

**DO:**
- Use framework language ("Here's how to replicate this...")
- Acknowledge trade-offs ("Higher yield = higher risk")
- Make comparisons explicit ("X has Y but lacks Z")
- Give credit to each stock's strengths
- Stay neutral (don't pick favorites unless data-driven)

**DON'T:**
- Try to cover each stock equally (focus on patterns)
- Include stocks just to hit a number (quality > quantity)
- Ignore the losers (why didn't others make the cut?)
- Present one dimension only (yield without risk = misleading)
- Claim "these are the ONLY stocks" (stay humble)

---

## ENGAGEMENT TACTICS FOR GROUP THREADS

**High engagement formats:**
- Polls: "Which would you choose: A, B, or C?"
- Tier ranking: "Put these in order for YOUR portfolio"
- Quiz: "Guess which stock has the highest [metric]"
- Debate: "Growth vs Yield - which camp are you?"

**Call-to-action options:**
- "Which stock would you pick?" (drives replies)
- "What did I miss?" (invites criticism/additions)
- "Own any of these?" (personal stories)
- "Would you add [X]?" (extends discussion)

**Timing considerations:**
- Post rankings Monday AM (start of week planning)
- Sector themes on sector rotation days
- Dividend themes before ex-dividend dates
- Avoid during major earnings weeks (too much noise)
