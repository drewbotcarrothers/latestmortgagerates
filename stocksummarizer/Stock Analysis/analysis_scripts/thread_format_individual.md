## THREAD STRUCTURE - INDIVIDUAL STOCK

### Tweet 1/15 - HOOK (Company-Specific)
Make it about THIS company, not the category.

**Hook Formulas:**
- **The Surprise**: "[Company] does [unexpected thing]. Here's why it matters: 🧵"
- **The Contradiction**: "Everyone thinks [common belief] about [Company]. The data tells a different story: 🧵"
- **The Milestone**: "[Company] just [achieved something rare]. I analyzed 10 years of data to understand how: 🧵"
- **The Hidden Gem**: "While everyone watches [Big Name], [Your Stock] quietly [impressive achievement] 🧵"

```
Example:

Royal Bank (RY) is Canada's largest bank by market cap.

But most investors miss this: It's grown dividends for 28 consecutive years while maintaining a sub-50% payout ratio.

That's not normal. Here's the deep dive: 🧵
```

## TONE & STYLE FOR INDIVIDUAL DEEP DIVES

**DO:**
- Use the company name (not just ticker) regularly
- Tell the business story, not just the numbers
- Include management/strategy context
- Acknowledge both strengths and weaknesses
- Compare to 2-3 direct competitors
- Provide historical context ("How did they get here?")

**DON'T:**
- Say "buy" or "sell" (frame as "for investors seeking X")
- Cherry-pick only positive data
- Ignore obvious risks
- Compare to irrelevant companies
- Rely solely on backward-looking metrics
- Make price predictions

---

## ENGAGEMENT TACTICS FOR INDIVIDUAL STOCKS

**Higher engagement when you:**
- Pick controversial names (banks, energy, telecom)
- Challenge consensus ("Everyone's wrong about...")
- Include management quotes or strategy changes
- Show math (CAGR calculations, total return breakdowns)
- Compare to a well-known US equivalent
- Time it to earnings releases or news events

**Moderate engagement for:**
- Consistent dividend payers (less drama)
- Small/mid caps (smaller audience)
- Non-controversial picks
- Purely fundamental analysis
