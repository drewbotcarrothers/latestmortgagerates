## TWEET 8/12 - WHY IS IT SO CHEAP?

Three reasons for the discount:

1. **Debt concerns**: $X billion debt load worries investors in high-rate environment

2. **Interest rate sensitivity**: Telecoms are rate-sensitive; BCE has significant leverage

3. **Competitive fears**: Rogers-Shaw merger created uncertainty about market dynamics

4. **Revenue growth**: Slowing subscriber growth = concerns about future cash flows

These are real risks. But are they $30+ per share risks?

---

## TWEET 9/12 - THE BEAR CASE (What Could Go Wrong)

Honest assessment of risks:

• **Debt servicing**: Rising rates increase interest expense, pressuring cash flow
• **Wireless competition**: Price wars could compress margins
• **Cord cutting**: TV subscriber losses accelerating
• **Regulatory risk**: CRTC decisions on wholesale rates
• **Dividend sustainability**: 90%+ payout ratio leaves little buffer

If rates stay elevated for years, the dividend could be at risk. That's the bear thesis.

---

## TWEET 10/12 - THE BULL RESPONSE

Why I think the bear case is overstated:

• **Rate peak**: Markets pricing in rate cuts in 2026; tailwind for leveraged companies
• **Inflation protection**: Telecom pricing power allows cost pass-through
• **Infrastructure moat**: Can't replicate Bell's network. Competitors hit capacity constraints.
• **Dividend coverage**: Free cash flow supports payout; management committed to dividend
• **Mean reversion**: 5x P/E is historically extreme for this business

The asymmetry favors longs at this price.
